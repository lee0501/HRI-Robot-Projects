PINKY BABE

An interactive robotic creature inspired by traffic cones and transformed into a friendly bird-like companion.

Overview

Pinky Babe is a dancing robot created to perform the song Golden.
The project integrates character design, motion choreography, and programming to enable the robot to dance in sync with the music.
By translating rhythm, timing, and expressive movements into robotic actions, Pinky Babe brings the energy and spirit of the song to life, showcasing the creative integration of technology and entertainment.

Background
The inspiration for this project stems from the song Golden. Known for its highly motivating lyrics, the track inherently captures the vibrant energy and essence of a virtual idol performance. Inspired by this synergy, we were challenged to establish our own idol from the ground up, designing a character capable of translating the song's dynamic rhythm into a cohesive live presentation.
Concept

A Friendly Bird-Like Companion

Character Development

The design process began with the shape of a traffic cone. Its pyramidal form provided a simple and recognizable structure that could easily accommodate the robot's internal mechanisms while maintaining stability during movement.

Because this triangular silhouette naturally echoed the posture of a sitting bird, we leaned into this organic form to transition the robot from a sterile mechanical object into a living character. To further enhance its endearing qualities, we introduced a baby-like face — softer facial features that helped create a more comforting and approachable personality while complementing the overall handcrafted aesthetic.

Material & Decoration

We chose multicolored yarn as the primary surface material because its rich color variations and woven texture create a visually vibrant appearance while conveying softness and warmth. By covering the mechanical structure with yarn, the robot feels less technological.

The robot also incorporates Bidens pilosa as a decorative element. Since the seeds naturally cling to fibrous surfaces, yarn provided an ideal material for attachment — allowing the plants to become part of the robot's exterior design.

Through the development of form, facial features, and material choices, the robot evolved from a simple traffic-cone-inspired structure into a playful bird-like character.

Choreography

Motion & Performance Design

Although the song Golden is known for its powerful rhythm and uplifting energy, our designated performance segment presented a contrast, featuring a relatively gentle and non-rhythmic structure. To ensure the character's performance harmonized with the music, we designed simple movements — such as the gentle flapping of the wings paired with the shifting brightness of the lights — to offer a fluid interpretation of this musical flow.

Furthermore, by mounting the robot onto a motorized vehicle, we achieved fully automated stage entrances and exits. The timing of these movements is precisely coordinated with musical transitions, shaping the pacing of the performance and maintaining a cohesive, immersive stage presentation.

Challenges

Encountered Problems & Solutions

Problem 01

Balancing Aesthetics and Functionality

Designing the robot's appearance required balancing aesthetics and functionality. The wings needed to align with the robot's movement path without affecting performance, while the yarn wrapping had to conceal internal mechanisms without interfering with operation.

Problem 02

Unpredictable Movement and Positioning

Due to interactions between the hardware and the environment, the robot's orientation could shift unpredictably during movement. To improve reliability, we conducted repeated on-site testing and adjusted starting positions and movement paths to keep the robot within the intended performance area.

Problem 03

Wear and Tear from Repeated Testing

Extensive testing gradually caused wear on the robot's structure. Wheels and fastening screws loosened over time, affecting movement accuracy and increasing directional drift. Exposed motor wires were also susceptible to damage, occasionally requiring re-soldering during the development process. These issues highlighted the importance of structural reinforcement and cable protection in future iterations.

Conclusion

Key Takeaways

Building a physical HRI project brought key insights into how audiences project empathy onto inanimate objects. When movement patterns match emotional cues, the robot's physical form transitions from a simple machine to a character with agency.

Behind the Scenes

One of our favorite discoveries happened unexpectedly. After attaching Bidens pilosa to the robot, we noticed that the dried plants transformed into beautiful star-shaped forms, giving the character an even more distinctive and expressive appearance.

We also visited NCCU's Maker Space to fabricate the robot's baby face using a laser cutter. Watching the layered acrylic components gradually come together was one of the most memorable moments of the entire building process.
