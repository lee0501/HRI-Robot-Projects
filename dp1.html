PINKY BABE

An interactive robotic creature inspired by traffic cones and transformed into a friendly bird-like companion.

Overview

Pinky Babe is a dancing robot created to perform the song Golden.
The project integrates character design, motion choreography, and programming to enable the robot to dance in sync with the music.
By translating rhythm, timing, and expressive movements into robotic actions, Pinky Babe brings the energy and spirit of the song to life, showcasing the creative integration of technology and entertainment.

Background
Developed as part of an Human-Robot Interaction (HRI) course project exploring how character design, motion choreography, and soft materials can create emotional connections between audiences and robot performers.
Concept

A Friendly Bird-Like Companion

Inspired by the shape of a traffic cone, the robot adopts a pyramidal silhouette as its primary form and reinterprets it as an adorably clumsy bird-like character. Through the use of soft materials and friendly facial features, the design reduces the mechanical appearance and creates a warm, comforting, and adorable character.

Details

Component Design Details

Surfac

Yarn is used to create a soft, approachable appearance inspired by plush toys. Its textured surface also supports the concept by allowing Bidens pilosa seeds to attach more easily.

Crest

A red flower crowns the robot's head, with a glowing light bulb at its center. This feature serves as a visual focal point, enhancing the character's recognizability and lively personality.

Wings

The wings use synchronized lighting to express movement and emotion, allowing the robot to visually respond to music and engage its audience. Exposed yarn details at the wing tips highlight the handmade quality, adding warmth and personality to the design.

Baby Face

The facial design adopts infant-like proportions to create a more endearing appearance. Combined with the transparent acrylic structure, the design feels lighter and balances bright colors with technological elements, reinforcing a gentle and therapeutic character identity.

Choreography

Motion & Performance Design

As the music features a relatively gentle and non-rhythmic structure, the robot's movements are designed to reflect the mood of the music rather than follow distinct beats. The flapping motion of the wings, combined with the changing brightness of the lights on both sides, creates a visual interpretation of the musical flow. The timing of the robot's entrance and exit is also coordinated with musical transitions, helping to shape the pacing of the performance and maintain a cohesive stage presentation.

Challenges

Encountered Problems & Solutions

Problem 01

Balancing Aesthetics and Functionality

Designing the robot's appearance required balancing aesthetics and functionality. The wings had to align with the robot's movement path without affecting performance, while the yarn wrapping was used to conceal the internal mechanisms. To refine these elements, we developed two design iterations before arriving at the final appearance.

Problem 02

Unpredictable Movement and Positioning

Due to the interaction between the hardware and the environment, the robot's orientation could shift unpredictably, making movement control challenging. To improve reliability, we tested multiple starting points and movement paths through repeated on-site trials, ensuring the robot remained within the intended area throughout the performance.

Problem 03

Wear and Tear from Repeated Testing

Repeated movement tests caused gradual wear on the robot's structure. The wheels and fastening screws loosened over time, increasing directional drift and affecting movement accuracy. Additionally, exposed motor wires were prone to damage, leading to wire breakage and requiring the motors to be re-soldered. These challenges revealed the need for stronger structural fixation and better cable protection.

Conclusion

Key Takeaways

Building a physical HRI project brought key insights into how audiences project empathy onto inanimate objects. When movement patterns match emotional cues, the robot's physical form transitions from a simple machine to a character with agency.

Fun Facts & Discoveries

One of our favorite discoveries happened by accident. After attaching Bidens pilosa to the robot, we noticed that the dried plants transformed into beautiful star-shaped forms, making the robot look even more unique and expressive.

We also visited NCCU's Maker Space, where we used a laser cutter to create the Baby Face. Watching the design come to life layer by layer was one of the most exciting parts of the building process.
