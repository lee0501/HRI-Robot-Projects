Character Exploration

Before developing the final Pink Babe prototype, we used clay and Styrofoam to explore different character concepts, creating six character prototypes.

The flexibility of clay allowed us to quickly test various appearances, proportions, and personalities. Compared to digital modeling or illustration, physical prototypes provide a more intuitive understanding of form, volume, and real-world scale.

These hands-on experiments helped us refine our ideas and establish a foundation for the final prototype.

Love Boxer

Love Boxer explores the contrast between love and heartbreak. The pink version represents confidence, happiness, and the excitement of being in love, while the gray version symbolizes vulnerability and emotional loss after a breakup.

Three-Legged Cat

Inspired by the Chinese idiom “three-legged cat,” a phrase describing someone with limited skills, we transformed the expression into a literal character—a cat with only three legs.

Cheese Egg

Cheese Egg combines the rounded form of an egg with cheese-inspired holes and colors. Fuzzy horns on its head add a playful touch, creating a whimsical character between food and fantasy.

Bella Bot — Scale Model Construction

Bella Bot is a well-known service robot. In this exercise, we used foamboard to build a precise scale solid model of Bella Bot based on online size references, in order to understand its structure, proportions and internal component hierarchy.

Intention

Physical models demand real-world awareness of scale, material, and structure that digital tools cannot replicate. Bella Bot's layered silhouette — stacked trays, cylindrical tower, wide base — made it an ideal subject for testing our ability to interpret and reconstruct complex form.

Process

Using foamboard for panels for structure, we gathered dimensional references online, divided responsibilities by component group, and assembled from the base upward — translating measurements into scaled cuts in parallel.

Challenge

Compounding errors emerged in assembly: panels from different groups didn't match, length-to-width ratios were off, and material thickness had never been factored in. Minor issues individually — significant structural deviation collectively.

What We Learned

A model's value is inseparable from its accuracy. Shared measurement standards, accounting for material thickness, and dry-fit tests before final assembly are the three practices we now treat as non-negotiable.
