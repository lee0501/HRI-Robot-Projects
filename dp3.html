Tomato Guardian

An emotional personal safety companion designed to provide reassurance and active protection through character transformations and smart sensing.

Overview

Tomato Guardian is a smart personal safety accessory designed for individuals who frequently travel alone, particularly at night or in unfamiliar environments. It integrates emergency assistance, location sharing, and threat detection into a compact wearable form, exploring how personal safety technology can be embedded into everyday life in a way that feels both practical and reassuring.

Background

Walking alone at night or navigating unfamiliar places can create feelings of anxiety and vulnerability. However, many personal safety products emphasize protection through forms that resemble defensive tools or emergency equipment, making them less appealing to carry on a daily basis. This raised an important question: How might safety devices provide reliable protection while remaining approachable, discreet, and suitable for everyday use?

Concept

Why Tomato?

The concept revolves around the duality of emotional companionship and active protection. During the exploration phase, three distinct form concepts were conceptualized, with the tomato chosen as the final design. By adopting a familiar, playful form, the product blends into daily life as an everyday accessory, reducing the anxiety often associated with carrying personal safety gear.


Beyond its approachable look, the tomato carries a strong visual narrative. Its red color evokes alertness, while its form allows for a clear metaphorical shift when danger is detected. By transitioning from a friendly companion to a vigilant guardian through expression and form, Tomato Guardian delivers reliable protection that feels responsive and deeply reassuring.

System States

Four Interactive Modes

01 / Normal Mode

Displays a friendly smile and serves as a decorative backpack charm while continuously monitoring the surroundings using infrared distance sensors.

02 / Alert Mode

Triggered by threat detection or manual activation. The face changes and leaves raise while the device emits high-decibel sirens, records video, and shares real-time GPS locations.

03 / Extended Mode I

Once the immediate danger subsides, the device remains on high alert and continues recording for a set period until the environment is confirmed completely safe.

04 / Extended Mode II

Activated if the threat persists. The device maintains its angry defense posture and directly calls the police.

Process

Design Iterations

01 / Expression Design — From a Friendly Companion to an Active Guardian

In the initial design, Tomato Guardian featured a single angry facial expression to emphasize its protective role. While this successfully communicated the product's purpose, it provided little indication of the device's operational status and offered no clear sense of state transition. To strengthen the interaction experience, the second iteration introduced two distinct facial expressions: a friendly expression for Normal Mode and an angry expression for Alert Mode. This visual transformation allows users to instantly recognize the device's current state while reinforcing the narrative of a companion that becomes an active guardian when danger is detected.

02 / Material Optimization — Redesigning the Leaves to Support Physical State Transformation

The first version used soft silicone for both the tomato body and its leaves. While this created a consistent appearance, the flexibility of the leaves made controlled shape transformation difficult and unreliable. To support the alert mode interaction, the leaves were redesigned using a rigid plastic material. When potential danger is detected, the leaves mechanically lift upward, creating a dramatic visual effect that reinforces the angry facial expression. This change not only improved the reliability of the transformation mechanism but also strengthened the device's ability to communicate its alert status through physical form.

03 / Mechanism — Introducing an Extension Mode for Smarter Protection

Early testing revealed that immediately triggering an emergency alarm after threat detection could result in false activations and unnecessary stress for users. To address this issue, we introduced an Extension Mode between Alert Mode and the emergency alarm. When suspicious activity is detected, the device first enters Alert Mode, activating its recording and monitoring functions. If the potential threat persists, it then transitions into Extension Mode before triggering the alarm. Even after the alarm is deactivated, the device continues recording and monitoring for a period of time to ensure ongoing threats can still be documented and tracked.

Reflection

Key Takeaways

Tomato Guardian was sparked when a team member found herself clutching her keys and phone during an unsettling night jog—a moment proving that safety is as much psychological as physical.

Our challenge lay in balancing hard technology with soft empathy. Rejecting the cold aesthetics of traditional safety gear, we integrated mechanical forms into an approachable companion that balances everyday usability with proactive defense.

Ultimately, this project redefined our design philosophy. True protection must align with human behavior and social context, ensuring technology empowers users without causing social awkwardness. Design should not merely react to a crisis; it must bridge the gap between functional necessity and human well-being.
